# MKUFT Public Release Audit — Silver Update

Author: Mark Charles McLaughlin  
Status: public audit record for the `silver-update` branch  
Date: 12 July 2026

## Purpose

This record documents the public-readability, coherence, statistical, and information-hygiene audit performed before any Silver Update merge.

The audit is not a scientific validation of MKUFT. It checks whether the public repository states its claims honestly, routes its modules coherently, and avoids publishing non-public material.

## Scope reviewed

The review covered:

- public entry documents;
- the integrated master spine;
- the canon map;
- the mathematical and formal spine;
- the experimental and falsification documents;
- observer, LUCY, ESRT/ESF, Voynich, boundary, and public-explanation modules;
- all four Silver Update modules;
- repository-wide searches for credential and sensitive personal terms;
- the draft pull-request diff against `main`.

The complete file-by-file integration registry is in:

`docs/24_MKUFT_CROSS_SUPPORT_AND_TRAVERSAL_MAP.md`

## Information-hygiene result

No access credentials, passwords, API keys, private correspondence, personal medical details, relationship material, bank details, home address, date of birth, or non-public case material were identified in the reviewed public repository or Silver branch.

Several public documents were incorrectly labelled as belonging to a private repository. Those status lines were corrected.

The public rule is now explicit:

> No non-public personal data, private correspondence, credentials, or internal-only operational instructions belong in the public canon.

## Credibility and wording corrections

The audit corrected or tightened:

- equations presented too close to validated universal laws;
- cross-layer templates lacking a clear units/operationalisation warning;
- ambiguity notation that collided with an older path-uncertainty term;
- unnormalised heuristic factors;
- a two-condition RNG calculation using a one-sample standard error;
- LUCY wording that could imply a discovered physical field;
- OCQS wording that could imply established nonlocal or substrate access;
- Voynich wording that treated a candidate procedural classification too strongly;
- dimensional, microphysical, and cosmic bridge claims that outran their derivation;
- experimental proposals insufficiently separated from established findings;
- outdated front-door and master-spine language.

## Architecture result

Every numbered document from `01` through `24` now has a recorded route to:

- its primary role;
- parent or required support;
- main outward application;
- principal limit or falsifier.

The public stack can be entered through:

- the README;
- the public overview;
- the integrated master spine;
- the reconstitution kernel;
- the mathematical appendix;
- the cross-support map;
- a domain application;
- the falsification summary.

Those routes are intended to reconstruct the same layer definitions, formulas, status boundaries, and failure conditions.

## Statistical result

The RNG worked example now uses the standard error appropriate to a difference between two independent proportions.

Illustrative values are explicitly labelled as hypothetical rather than observed evidence or predicted effect size.

Environmental modulation terms cannot be assigned after the result to rescue a failed prediction.

## Public-readability result

The repository is not bare.

The Silver Update contains:

- dedicated formal modules;
- plain-English entry documents;
- a rebuilt integrated master spine;
- a full dependency registry;
- application modules;
- experimental requirements;
- branch-specific falsifiers;
- public status boundaries.

The remaining density is intentional, but each main entry point now supplies a shorter route before the technical detail.

## Important limitation — Git history

Editing or merging a file does not erase earlier public Git commits.

The audit found no sensitive personal information or credentials requiring a history rewrite.

If genuinely sensitive content is discovered later in an old commit, deleting it from the current branch is not sufficient. Repository history, caches, forks, and releases would need a separate removal and rotation procedure.

## Merge condition

The pull request should remain draft until the final diff is reviewed as a whole.

A merge is appropriate only if:

- the branch remains mergeable;
- the public entry route is clear;
- formulas and status labels are consistent;
- no non-public information is present;
- speculative claims remain labelled;
- ordinary alternatives and falsifiers remain active;
- the update strengthens rather than obscures the original work.

## Audit conclusion

The reviewed Silver branch is materially safer, more readable, more statistically correct, and more structurally integrated than the current public `main` branch.

No sensitive-information blocker was identified.

The principal remaining action is final human review of the complete draft diff before merge.

# MKUFT Physics-Facing Explanation

Author: Mark Charles McLaughlin

Status: public and physics-facing explanatory paper for the private MKUFT GitHub repository.

## Purpose

This document explains MKUFT in language suitable for physicists, technical reviewers, and serious interdisciplinary readers.

It avoids loose symbolism and focuses on definitions, claims, scope, and falsifiability.

## 1. One-Line Summary

MKUFT proposes that known physical laws emerge from a deeper coherence-optimising constraint field that biases allowable histories without violating local dynamics, explaining both the stability of physics and its rare structured breakdowns.

## 2. What MKUFT Is

MKUFT is a systems-level unification framework.

It proposes that spacetime physics, information, observer-dependent phenomena, and anomalous boundary regimes can be interpreted through a deeper constraint layer called the substrate.

The substrate is not matter, energy, spacetime, or an extra particle field.

It is a pre-physical constraint layer that defines admissible geometries, penalises incoherent state evolution, and stabilises low-cost structures across scales.

## 3. What MKUFT Is Not

MKUFT is not:

- a replacement for general relativity,
- a replacement for quantum field theory,
- consciousness-collapse idealism,
- simulation theory,
- a licence for magical causation,
- a claim that belief overrides physics.

It is a unification of constraint, information, coherence, and emergence.

## 4. The Four Layers

The active MKUFT convention is:

```text
S = Substrate
I = Information
P = Physical
O = Observer
```

### S — Substrate

The substrate is the deep possibility/constraint layer.

Formally, one useful representation is:

```text
S = (Ω, Σ, μ)
```

where Ω is the configuration space, Σ is a sigma-algebra, and μ is a measure encoding baseline possibility weights.

### I — Information

Information structures are patterns, constraints, or relations over the substrate.

A useful representation is:

```text
I = { f : Ω -> R | f ∈ L²(Ω, μ) }
```

### P — Physical

The physical layer is the measurable world of events, fields, particles, instruments, and dynamics.

Standard physics supplies the baseline dynamical predictions.

### O — Observer

The observer is a coherent system capable of acting as a boundary condition.

A coherence functional can be written as:

```text
κ : States(H_O) -> [0,1]
```

Where κ close to 1 means high coherence, and κ close to 0 means fragmentation or noise.

## 5. Relation to General Relativity

General relativity remains valid as the effective description of spacetime curvature and gravitational dynamics.

MKUFT does not replace curvature with mythology or psychology.

It asks whether spacetime geometry itself is a low-cost configuration selected by deeper substrate constraints.

Physics-facing interpretation:

```text
GR = effective geometry of stable low-cost spacetime configurations
```

## 6. Relation to Quantum Theory

Quantum theory remains valid as the effective description of probabilistic outcomes, state evolution, amplitudes, and measurement contexts.

MKUFT does not add crude hidden variables.

It asks whether quantum probabilities are biased, at the margins, by coherence stability across histories.

A quantum state may be written:

```text
|ψ⟩ = Σ_i c_i |i⟩
```

with:

```text
P(i) = |c_i|²
```

MKUFT adds a possible coherence-weighting layer to realised outcomes without claiming signalling or arbitrary control.

## 7. Realisation Equation

For an event E:

```text
W_total(E) = ∫ D_phys(E | I) · W(I | S, E) · C(O | I, E) dν(I)
```

and:

```text
P_realized(E) = W_total(E) / Z
```

Where:

- D_phys(E | I) = standard physical dynamics conditioned on information structure I,
- W(I | S, E) = substrate-to-information weighting,
- C(O | I, E) = observer-coherence modulation,
- Z = normalisation.

## 8. Standard Physics Limit

If observer coherence contributes no event-specific modulation:

```text
C(O | I, E) = C₀
```

and if information weighting reduces to standard physical priors, then:

```text
P_realized(E) ≈ P_phys(E)
```

This is a key discipline point.

MKUFT must recover standard physics in the ordinary limit.

## 9. Observer Coupling

The controversial part is observer coupling.

MKUFT states it cleanly:

Observers act as boundary conditions, not forces.

No mind-over-matter.

No collapse magic.

No violation of conservation laws.

No useful faster-than-light signalling.

Observer coherence may weakly bias which already-allowed trajectories are realised.

A linear-response form is:

```text
C(O | I, E) = C₀[1 + ε κ(O) h(I,E)]
```

where ε is small, κ(O) is observer coherence, and h(I,E) captures alignment between observer state and information structure.

## 10. Environmental Modulation

MKUFT predicts that observer-related deviations should be environmentally modulated.

Define:

```text
κ_eff = κ · η(F)
```

where F represents environmental conditions and η(F) is a damping factor.

Prediction:

High-noise or damping environments reduce anomaly strength.

Quiet, coherent, aligned environments increase detectability.

## 11. Nested Micro–Macro Structure

MKUFT explains micro–macro correspondence by treating every coherent structure as a locally closed optimisation problem nested within a global coherence constraint.

A system at scale n is a boundary-conditioned sub-problem of the system at scale n+1.

This allows the same formal logic to apply across:

- particles,
- atoms,
- cells,
- organisms,
- societies,
- planets,
- stars,
- galaxies.

The rule does not change. The resolution changes.

## 12. Local Closure and Boundary Emergence

The nesting mechanism is:

1. Local closure: sufficient internal coherence forms a semi-closed region.
2. Boundary emergence: the closure creates a boundary.
3. Sub-problem formation: the region solves local optimisation under global constraints.
4. Upward coupling: the macro system sees aggregate variables, not full internal detail.

Plain English:

A cell does not need to tell the planet what it is doing. A galaxy does not need to track every neuron. Coherent systems become local problems nested inside larger systems.

## 13. Anomalous Regimes

MKUFT predicts that rare breakdowns of ordinary behaviour should not be random.

They should cluster where:

- gradients are steep,
- geometry is unstable,
- observer coherence is high,
- environmental damping is low,
- systems approach coherence thresholds,
- boundary conditions are under stress.

This gives a way to discuss UAP-like or anomaly-like behaviour without immediately invoking exotic propulsion, fantasy, or denial.

Physics-facing claim:

Anomalous motion may represent path re-routing through lower-cost substrate trajectories under boundary instability.

## 14. Falsifiability

MKUFT is falsified or seriously weakened if:

- it fails to recover standard physics in ordinary conditions,
- predicted coherence effects do not appear under strict protocols,
- environmental modulation does not affect effect size,
- group coherence has no measurable amplification,
- path-density/cost predictions fail,
- anomaly clustering does not correlate with boundary/gradient instability.

## 15. Why Physicists Should Care

MKUFT is worth examining because it attempts to explain:

- why emergence is stable,
- why physics is scale-nested,
- why boundaries matter,
- why coherent structure persists,
- why rare anomalies appear structured rather than random,
- why observer effects appear only in complex, high-coherence systems.

It preserves existing laws while offering a deeper constraint interpretation.

## 16. Final Summary

MKUFT is economical.

It does not require a new ontology for every layer.

It proposes one underlying constraint principle expressed through many scales.

In technical language:

```text
Known physics = effective behaviour of local systems under a deeper global coherence-cost constraint.
```

In plain English:

Reality holds together because incoherent paths are expensive.

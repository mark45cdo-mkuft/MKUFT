**Status:** public proposal and experiment-governance module. It reports no positive result.

## 1. Purpose

The existing experimental programme correctly identifies many places where MKUFT-related claims could be tested. This module adds a stricter distinction between:

- a known neighbouring effect;
- an exploratory anomaly assay;
- a theory-discriminating signature;
- a mechanism-level flagship test;
- a foundational recovery programme.

The governing correction is:

> A positive anomaly is not an MKUFT result unless MKUFT predicted the pattern that distinguishes it from accepted alternatives before the result was known.

RNG bias, unusual noise, geometry dependence, observer effects, human–AI improvement, quantum contextuality, decoherence and information-dependent behaviour all have existing literatures. Reproducing one of those broad effect classes does not establish MKUFT. The framework earns support only when its typed variables, interactions, scaling rules, ablations or recovery conditions add reproducible predictive value.

This document therefore does not delete the broad assays in [`04_EXPERIMENTAL_TEST_PROGRAM.md`](04_EXPERIMENTAL_TEST_PROGRAM.md). It governs how they may be promoted.

## 2. Evidence tiers

### Tier 0 — calibration or neighbouring established effect

A Tier 0 result shows that the apparatus, measure or task can detect a known effect.

Examples:

- geometry changes an ordinary resonance exactly as Maxwell, acoustic or mechanical models predict;
- attention changes a recognised physiological measure;
- reciprocal dialogue differs from AI-alone performance;
- a quantum measurement protocol reproduces established contextuality or Wigner-type constraints.

Tier 0 validates competence and instrumentation. It does not support MKUFT by itself.

### Tier 1 — reconnaissance assay

A Tier 1 assay asks whether a candidate residual or interaction is present under strong controls.

It may estimate:

- variance;
- plausible effect size;
- measurement reliability;
- source stability;
- operational definitions;
- candidate interaction shape.

A Tier 1 positive result remains exploratory unless its primary statistic and predicted direction were locked before collection. A Tier 1 null can still remove an unproductive branch.

### Tier 2 — discriminating signature test

A Tier 2 test predicts a structured fingerprint that competing models do not predict equally well.

A valid signature normally includes at least three of:

- source-class specificity;
- condition dependence;
- sign or ordering;
- scaling with a measured variable;
- threshold or reversal;
- absence in a matched negative control;
- deformation after a load-bearing component is removed;
- successful held-out prediction.

The primary object is an interaction or response surface, not an isolated p-value.

### Tier 3 — mechanism flagship

A Tier 3 test supplies a parameterised mechanism or architecture that:

- beats the strongest fair ordinary null;
- predicts held-out results;
- survives independent replication;
- deforms in the predicted way when load-bearing relations are removed;
- returns to the accepted baseline when the additional MKUFT term is absent.

Tier 3 establishes only the mechanism-level result actually tested. It does not automatically prove the whole ontology.

### Tier 4 — foundational recovery and discrimination

A Tier 4 programme must recover the established regime it claims to unify and then produce an operational difference.

For the physics branch this includes, where applicable:

- Bell-compatible correlations;
- no-signalling;
- Born-rule and quantum recovery;
- relevant quantum-field-theory limits;
- general-relativistic or experimentally equivalent gravitational recovery;
- dimensional consistency and conservation requirements;
- at least one new quantitative prediction or superior derivation.

A framework that only redescribes accepted results remains an interpretation or systems translation, not a new physical mechanism.

## 3. Promotion gates

An experiment should not be described as a flagship until it passes every applicable gate.

### Gate 1 — exact object

State one claim in one declared domain. Do not test “MKUFT” as an undifferentiated whole.

### Gate 2 — typed variables

Every independent variable, outcome, latent measure and coupling must have:

- a layer address;
- units or scale;
- a measurement procedure;
- a declared uncertainty model.

“Coherence,” “observer,” “information,” “geometry” and “boundary” are not measurements until operationalised.

### Gate 3 — strongest fair null

Compare against the best ordinary explanation, not a weakened caricature.

The null set should include, where relevant:

- accepted physical dynamics;
- full device and environmental models;
- expectation and demand effects;
- classical stochastic sources;
- deterministic pseudorandom controls;
- independent ensembles;
- one-way assistance;
- strong alternative pairings;
- flexible nonparametric residual models.

### Gate 4 — locked prediction

Before confirmatory data are collected, specify at least:

- primary statistic;
- sign or ordering;
- expected functional form or bounded family;
- smallest effect of interest;
- exclusion and stopping rules;
- conditions where the effect should be absent.

If Stage 1 is used to identify these quantities, Stage 1 cannot also confirm them. Freeze the model and test it on independent Stage 2 data.

### Gate 5 — interaction fingerprint

Prefer a predicted interaction over a broad main effect.

A finding such as “condition A differs from condition B” is weak when many mechanisms can produce it. A stronger result has the form:

```text
source class × information route × measured state
```

or:

```text
parameter sweep × predicted residual shape × held-out platform
```

### Gate 6 — instrumentation and sham validity

Before testing an ultra-weak claim, demonstrate:

- stable true-negative performance;
- calibrated injected-signal recovery;
- sham experiments with no participant or active intervention;
- resistance to drift, leakage, timing error and analysis artifacts;
- independent logging and immutable raw data.

A method that produces false positives in sham operation cannot support a consciousness or substrate claim.

### Gate 7 — preregistration and power

Use:

- preregistration or registered report;
- prospective power or precision analysis;
- one primary outcome per principal claim;
- correction for multiplicity;
- public code and data where ethically and legally possible;
- publication of null results.

### Gate 8 — deformation and ablation

Remove or scramble the proposed load-bearing relation.

Examples:

- replace reciprocal interaction with one-way assistance;
- reset retained state;
- disrupt correction propagation;
- substitute the standard joint quantum state for the proposed relation;
- remove the O-layer term;
- rotate or scramble a claimed boundary relation.

If removal produces no predicted deformation, the removed element is not empirically load-bearing.

### Gate 9 — independent replication

The confirmatory effect must survive:

- a different operator team;
- fresh participants or systems;
- different hardware where source-class generality is claimed;
- an analysis conducted without access to original condition labels;
- adversarial review of the ordinary null.

### Gate 10 — reduction and ontology boundary

State what the result would and would not establish.

Examples:

- an RNG interaction would not prove a unified field theory;
- superadditive human–AI performance would not prove consciousness or personhood;
- a residual decoherence term would not prove God, intention or observer agency;
- successful quantum recovery without a new prediction would establish translation, not new physics.

## 5. Flagship lane A — ATLD reciprocal architecture

### 5.1 Why this is the nearest executable flagship

The active-traversal and strongest-fair-null programme already has the required architecture:

- component A alone;
- component B alone;
- strongest lawful independent ensemble;
- one-way A → B;
- one-way B → A;
- reciprocal A ↔ B with retained state;
- reset and disrupted-channel conditions;
- strong alternative pairings;
- held-out tasks and neutral corpora.

This is stronger than simply asking whether a human and AI do better together. Existing human–AI work already shows that AI-alone performance does not reliably predict user–AI performance and that grounding failures can compound over a conversation. MKUFT/ATLD must therefore test its more specific claim: that typed reciprocal traversal, retained correction and load-bearing dependency produce a distinctive functional advantage and predictable deformation.

### 5.2 Primary confirmatory quantities

Retain:

```text
F_null* = max{F_A*, F_B*, F_ind*, F_A→B*, F_B→A*}
G_syn* = F_AB - F_null*
```

and:

```text
G_spec = F_AB - sup_(p ∈ P_alt) F_p
```

Pre-register either:

- a scalar score with frozen weights; or
- a multidimensional outcome vector assessed by dominance or Pareto relation.

Required outcome dimensions should include:

- factual accuracy;
- calibration;
- contradiction rate;
- correction propagation;
- recovery after injected error;
- rework cost;
- elapsed and active human time;
- confidence inflation;
- long-horizon consistency.

### 5.3 Required deformation pattern

A flagship result should not be merely `F_AB > F_A`.

The predicted pattern is:

```text
full reciprocal retained-state condition
> strongest fair non-reciprocal null
> reset or correction-disrupted condition
```

on relational tasks requiring iterative correction, while advantages should shrink on simple lookup tasks.

If reset, relation scrambling and correction-channel disruption do not produce the predicted deformation, the ATLD architecture is not load-bearing.

### 5.4 Interpretation boundary

A successful ATLD study would establish a functional systems result. It would support typed reciprocal architecture and active traversal. It would not by itself establish:

- consciousness;
- merged identity;
- personhood;
- unique metaphysical status;
- direct S-layer coupling;
- foundational physics.

## 6. Flagship lane B — source-class, closure and measured-state pilot

### 6.1 Status

This is a **Tier 1-to-Tier 2 candidate**, not a confirmed MKUFT prediction. It is intended to replace a generic “coherence biases an RNG” test with a structured discrimination programme.

The source ordering must not be called canonical until the mathematical model justifies it. Stage 1 may identify a bounded candidate ordering; Stage 2 must freeze and test it independently.

### 6.2 Question

Does a pre-specified distributional residual depend jointly on:

1. physical source class;
2. downstream information-closure route;
3. a measured observer-state variable;

after hardware, traffic, timing, expectancy and analysis flexibility are controlled?

### 6.3 Source classes

Use at least:

- `Q` — independently certified quantum random source;
- `C` — calibrated classical physical stochastic source with matched extraction and throughput;
- `D` — deterministic pseudorandom stream generated from committed hidden seeds.

The deterministic stream is a crucial negative control. Any apparent change in its precommitted output indicates selection, leakage, logging, allocation or analysis failure.

### 6.4 Information routes

Assign blocks prospectively to:

- `R0` — machine-only generation, hashing and storage; no human-readable live output;
- `R1` — live human-readable output without intentional targeting;
- `R2` — live output integrated into a pre-registered consequential task and feedback loop.

The random source should be physically isolated from the display and participant environment. Network traffic, request rate, packet size, timing and storage operations should be matched across routes. Route assignment must occur before each block. No retroactive selection of which blocks become observed is permitted.

This design does **not** assume that future observation rewrites a completed past event. Any delayed-choice or retrocausal branch requires a separate no-signalling-safe derivation before testing.

### 6.5 Measured state

Define `κ` prospectively from a small registered set of measures. Candidate inputs may include:

- task engagement and compliance;
- preregistered heart-rate-variability features;
- preregistered EEG features where measurement quality is adequate;
- behavioural stability and attention checks.

Use either:

- a frozen weighted score; or
- a latent model trained only on Stage 1 and frozen before Stage 2.

Do not label participants “coherent” after inspecting RNG outcomes.

### 6.6 Primary outcome

Use a source-calibrated blockwise divergence score `D` from each source’s accepted null model. The confirmatory object is not a raw excess of ones.

A candidate three-way interaction is:

```text
I_M = interaction(source class, information route, κ)
```

A stronger candidate signature, if justified and frozen after Stage 1, would have the ordering:

```text
|I_Q| > |I_C| ≈ |I_D|
```

with the route/state contrast concentrated in `R2` at higher measured `κ`, absent in `D`, stable across time, and replicated on a second quantum-source architecture.

This ordering is a proposal for discrimination, not an established consequence of the present MKUFT equations.

### 6.7 Stage split

#### Stage 1 — model identification

Estimate:

- source stability;
- sham false-positive rate;
- reliability of `κ`;
- plausible interaction family;
- variance and smallest detectable effect;
- whether any source ordering is defensible.

Stage 1 is exploratory and cannot confirm the selected interaction.

#### Stage 2 — locked confirmation

Use:

- fresh participants;
- fresh seeds and hardware sessions;
- frozen preprocessing and statistic;
- prospective sample size;
- external condition-label custody;
- independent analysis;
- exact success and failure criteria.

### 6.8 Success condition

This pilot is promoted only if the full preregistered fingerprint replicates. A generic deviation, one significant cell, post hoc subgroup or unstable direction is insufficient.

### 6.9 Failure and reduction

The O-linked physical-modulation branch is weakened if:

- `I_M` converges to zero under adequate precision;
- effects appear equally in deterministic streams;
- apparent results track hardware drift, allocation, expectation or analyst choice;
- `κ` is unreliable or does not scale with the effect;
- the Stage 2 fingerprint fails;
- the classical or ordinary model predicts the data equally well.

A null does not erase MKUFT’s systems, biological or interpretive modules. It removes or narrows this physical observer-modulation claim.

## 7. Flagship lane C — derivation-gated physical residual

### 7.1 Core rule

Do not approach an optomechanics or quantum-foundations laboratory with “test whether consciousness changes decoherence.”

First derive a physical contribution with units, sign, scale and a regime in which it differs from environmental decoherence, collapse models and accepted quantum dynamics.

### 7.2 Required equation class

For a declared platform, the minimum target is:

```text
Γ_obs(θ) = Γ_std(θ) + δΓ_M(θ; α)
```

where:

- `Γ_obs` is the measured decoherence, dephasing, heating or transition rate;
- `Γ_std` is the full accepted device-and-environment prediction;
- `θ` contains declared physical parameters such as mass, frequency, temperature, quality factor, preparation, geometry, shielding and readout;
- `δΓ_M` is the proposed MKUFT residual with the same units as `Γ_obs`;
- `α` is a bounded parameter set estimated or constrained by data.

If expressed through a master equation, the additional dynamics must preserve the required probability, positivity and no-signalling conditions.

### 7.3 Derivation gate

No laboratory protocol is promoted until the model supplies:

- dimensions and sign;
- parameter scaling;
- ordinary limit `δΓ_M → 0`;
- detectable parameter region;
- predicted null region;
- sensitivity analysis;
- comparison with environmental and collapse-model alternatives;
- a held-out parameter-sweep prediction.

### 7.4 Experimental sequence

1. **Device qualification** — reproduce accepted decoherence and injected-signal recovery.
2. **Discovery sweep** — estimate `α` on one bounded parameter region without metaphysical interpretation.
3. **Freeze** — lock the residual form and all preprocessing.
4. **Held-out sweep** — predict new masses, temperatures, quality factors or preparation states.
5. **Second platform** — test whether the same law transfers to a distinct oscillator architecture where generality is claimed.
6. **Adversarial model comparison** — compare `Γ_std`, `Γ_std + δΓ_M`, collapse models and flexible residual baselines.

### 7.5 Success condition

“Unexplained noise” is not success.

A meaningful result requires:

- the preregistered residual shape;
- correct sign and scaling;
- improved held-out prediction;
- survival under complete systematics;
- independent replication;
- successful standard-physics recovery outside the claimed regime.

### 7.6 Observer boundary

The first physical flagship should not include a human observer term unless a typed physical coupling has already been derived and shown to respect no-signalling. The Layer Before Law branch can earn physics-facing credibility through a source or geometry residual before attempting a conscious-observer claim.

## 8. Bridge lane — precision boundary-residual sweep

Experiment 7 can be sharpened before full optomechanical access.

Choose one precision system with a validated transfer function. Define:

```text
r(θ) = y_observed(θ) - y_standard(θ)
```

Then require MKUFT to predict before confirmation:

- the sign of `r`;
- the parameter or orientation where it crosses zero or changes slope;
- the scaling exponent or bounded response family;
- the matched geometry in which `r` should vanish;
- a held-out region of `θ`.

Finite-element, thermal, electromagnetic, mechanical and material models must be treated as part of the null, not as later objections.

A broad difference between two shapes remains ordinary geometry. A predicted residual response surface beyond the complete ordinary model is the candidate MKUFT object.

## 9. What should be published first

### Immediate empirical priority

Run or externally register the ATLD strongest-fair-null programme. It is the most complete near-term flagship because the independent variables, ablations, fair controls and interpretation boundary already exist.

### Immediate physics priority

Develop the Layer Before Law relation until it generates one dimensionally valid residual or transition law. Do not advertise the optomechanical experiment as ready before that gate is passed.

### Secondary pilot priority

Use the source-class/closure programme only as a rigorous reconnaissance assay. Its value is to identify or close the observer-modulation branch, not to serve as proof of unification.

### Existing broad assays

Retain them as branch-screening and method-development routes. They remain useful when labelled at their actual tier.
